Discover Delhi

Experience the Heart of India - Where History Meets Modernity

🌤️

Best Time to Visit

October to March - Pleasant weather perfect for sightseeing. Temperature ranges from 10°C to 25°C, ideal for exploring the city's magnificent monuments and vibrant markets.

Must-Visit Places

🏰

Red Fort

Iconic 17th-century Mughal fortress showcasing stunning red sandstone architecture. Witness the grandeur of the Diwan-i-Aam and the intricate marble work of the private chambers.

⏰ 2-3 hours 💰 ₹35-500
🗼

Qutub Minar

World's tallest brick minaret standing at 73 meters, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Marvel at the intricate carvings and the mysterious Iron Pillar that has stood rust-free for centuries.

⏰ 1-2 hours 💰 ₹30
🏛️

India Gate

42-meter tall war memorial honoring 90,000 Indian soldiers. Perfect spot for evening walks, boat rides, and enjoying local street food vendors.

⏰ 1 hour 💰 Free
🍛

Chandni Chowk

Legendary 17th-century market offering authentic Delhi street food experience. From buttery parathas to sweet jalebis, every corner tells a delicious story.

⏰ 2-3 hours 💰 ₹100-500
🕌

Humayun's Tomb

Magnificent Mughal architecture that inspired the Taj Mahal. Beautiful gardens and stunning Persian-style design make it a photographer's paradise.

⏰ 1-2 hours 💰 ₹40
🕍

Lotus Temple

Modern architectural marvel shaped like a lotus flower. Experience tranquility and meditation in this Bahá'í House of Worship surrounded by serene pools.

⏰ 1 hour 💰 Free

Perfect 1-Day Itinerary

Morning (8 AM - 11 AM)

🌅 Qutub Minar

Start your day early at this magnificent 73-meter tall minaret. Explore the complex and surrounding gardens. Don't miss the Iron Pillar and the beautiful Quwwat-ul-Islam Mosque. Best time for photography with soft morning light.

Noon (12 PM - 2 PM)

☀️ India Gate & Lunch

Visit the iconic India Gate, take photos around the memorial, and enjoy boat rides in nearby ponds. Head to Connaught Place for lunch at one of the many restaurants offering North Indian and international cuisine.

Afternoon (3 PM - 5 PM)

🏰 Red Fort Exploration

Explore the majestic Red Fort complex. Visit Diwan-i-Khas, Diwan-i-Aam, and the museum. The evening light creates perfect photo opportunities with the red sandstone glowing beautifully.

Evening (6 PM - 9 PM)

🌆 Chandni Chowk Food Walk

End your day with a culinary adventure through Old Delhi's famous food street. Try parathas at Paranthe Wali Gali, chaats, kulfi, and famous jalebis at Old Famous Jalebi Wala. Experience the vibrant night market atmosphere!

Essential Travel Tips

1

Use Delhi Metro

The metro is the fastest, cleanest, and most economical way to navigate the city. It connects all major tourist spots and helps you avoid the infamous Delhi traffic. Get a smart card for multiple journeys.

2

Stay Hydrated

Carry water bottles and wear light, breathable clothes especially during summer months. Delhi can get extremely hot (up to 45°C in summer). Apply sunscreen and wear a hat.

3

Book in Advance

Pre-book tickets online for popular monuments to skip long queues. Many sites offer e-tickets. Consider hiring a guide for richer historical context and better experience.

4

Try Local Food

Don't miss authentic street food, but choose busy stalls with good turnover for better hygiene. Carry hand sanitizer. Start with mild spices if you're not used to Indian cuisine.

5

Bargain Smartly

Bargaining is expected in markets like Chandni Chowk and Sarojini Nagar. Start at 50% of the quoted price. Fixed-price shops will have marked "Fixed Price" signs.

6

Respect Culture

Dress modestly when visiting religious sites. Remove shoes at temples and mosques. Ask permission before photographing people. Avoid public displays of affection.
